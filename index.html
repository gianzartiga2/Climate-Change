Climate Change: Understanding the Crisis

Rising temperatures, damaged ecosystems, and extreme weather are already reshaping lives. Understanding the causes is the first step toward practical action.

Learn More Take Action
Causes

What drives climate change?

Human activities release heat-trapping gases and reduce the planet's natural ability to absorb carbon.

CO2

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Burning coal, oil, and gas releases carbon dioxide and methane that trap heat in the atmosphere.

TREE

Deforestation

Clearing forests removes carbon-absorbing trees and often releases stored carbon back into the air.

IND

Industrial Pollution

Factories, cement production, and heavy manufacturing consume energy and emit climate-warming gases.

AG

Agriculture

Livestock, rice farming, fertilizer use, and land conversion contribute methane and nitrous oxide.

CAR

Transportation

Cars, trucks, ships, and planes powered by fossil fuels produce significant global emissions.

Effects

How climate change affects people and nature

The impacts are connected, multiplying risks for communities, economies, health, and ecosystems.

HOT

Rising Temperatures

Heat waves are becoming more frequent and intense, increasing stress on people, crops, and energy systems.

SEA

Sea Level Rise

Melting ice and warming oceans threaten coastal cities, islands, homes, and freshwater supplies.

WX

Extreme Weather

Stronger storms, heavier rainfall, droughts, and wildfires create higher disaster risks.

BIO

Biodiversity Loss

Changing habitats put plants, animals, pollinators, coral reefs, and food webs under pressure.

HLTH

Health Impacts

Heat illness, poor air quality, disease spread, and food insecurity can affect public health.

Mitigation Projects

Solutions that reduce emissions

Climate mitigation combines clean energy, nature restoration, smarter cities, and circular resource use.

Renewable energy

Solar and Wind Farm Network

Community-scale solar arrays and wind turbines replace fossil fuel electricity with clean power.

Strategy: Decarbonize electricity generation and expand battery storage.

Reforestation

Native Forest Recovery Program

Local volunteers restore degraded land with native trees that protect wildlife and watersheds.

Strategy: Increase carbon absorption and rebuild resilient ecosystems.

Clean mobility

Sustainable Transport Initiative

Bike lanes, electric buses, and walkable streets help communities reduce car dependence.

Strategy: Cut transport emissions through efficient, low-carbon travel.

R3

Waste Reduction and Recycling Program

Composting, repair hubs, material recovery, and education reduce landfill methane and resource demand.

Strategy: Build a circular economy that keeps materials in use longer.

BLDG

Green Building Project

Efficient insulation, rooftop gardens, LED lighting, and smart cooling lower building energy use.

Strategy: Improve energy efficiency and use climate-friendly design.

Every action helps shape a safer climate future.

Support climate education, choose cleaner transportation, reduce waste, plant and protect trees, and share reliable information with your community.
